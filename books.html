Books

I write these stories for myself—out of the simple impulse to take worlds I enjoy and explore the versions that make the most sense to me. When I connect with a piece of media, I almost always end up imagining different angles: threads that could have gone deeper, character choices that deserved more weight, or worldbuilding that feels richer with a bit more structure.

These projects aren’t meant for publication. They’re personal rewrites and narrative experiments—places where I can pull apart a story, rebuild it, and see how it changes when the logic is strengthened or the characters are given room to grow. Some ideas stay close to the source; others drift into something new. What connects them all is the freedom to write without deadlines, expectations, or the constraints of canon.

This section collects the worlds I’m currently exploring: reimagined versions of familiar stories, and a few original ones shaped by the same curiosity. They’re not final products—they’re long-form creative spaces where I learn, experiment, and build the stories I want to read.

The 100 Rewrite

A reimagining of The 100 that rebuilds its world from the ground up—focusing on coherent science, stronger character motivations, and a clearer chain of events leading into the original story. This version expands on the political tensions, technological decisions, and personal flaws that shape the apocalypse, exploring how small choices accumulate into the world we eventually see.

The Harry Potter Rewrite

A dark, grounded story set centuries before the Harry Potter era, following a wandering healer whose search for a cure to the plague leads him into the origins of the Deathly Hallows. Blending medieval history, time travel, wandlore, and the cost of magic, the story explores how one man’s grief shapes the future of wizardkind.

Danny the Street

An original mystery narrated by someone who is not entirely alive, set in a town whose streets behave like sentient beings. Blending subtle surrealism with quiet humor, the story follows a narrator trying to make sense of a place that rearranges itself, hides secrets, and reacts to the people living in it.
