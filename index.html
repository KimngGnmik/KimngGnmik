Siddhanta Shrestha ｜ सिद्धान्त श्रेष्ठ

I'm a Controls Engineer fascinated by the intersection of physics, software, and intelligent systems. At Caterpillar, I design and validate embedded control systems for heavy machinery, working across the full development lifecycle—from Simulink model design and software integration to rigorous bench testing. My work focuses on creating robust control architectures that adapt to uncertainty while maintaining reliability.

With a foundation in Computer Engineering from UMass Amherst (B.S. and M.S.), I’ve developed expertise in embedded systems and real-time software, driven by the challenge of connecting theoretical principles to real-world impact. This mindset extends to my work mentoring the FTC robotics team Fast but not Furious (FBNF), where I guide students through trajectory planning, debugging, and systems integration.

What drives me is the belief that today’s control engineering challenges—whether in machinery, robotics, or future space systems—are built on the same principles of intelligent adaptation and dependable performance. I’m particularly passionate about closed-loop and adaptive control systems, and I see them as key technologies that will push the boundaries of what’s possible—from Earth to space.
